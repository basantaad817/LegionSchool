Harry Potter is a series of seven fantasy novels written by British author J. K. Rowling. The novels chronicle the lives of a young wizard named Harry Potter and his friends Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, all of whom are students at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. The main story arc concerns Harry's struggle against Lord Voldemort, a dark wizard who intends to become immortal, overthrow the wizard governing body known as the Ministry of Magic, and subjugate all wizards and Muggles (non-magical people)

Black Holes

A black hole is a region of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, including light can escape from its gravitational pull. Black holes are formed when massive stars collapse under their own gravity at the end of their life cycle. As matter is squeezed into a tiny volume, it creates a gravitational field so intense that even particles traveling at the speed of light cannot break free. The boundary surrounding a black hole is called the event horizon, beyond which nothing can return. Once an object crosses the event horizon, it is trapped within the black hole's gravitational grip, unable to communicate with the outside universe.
